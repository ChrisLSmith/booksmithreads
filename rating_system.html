How I Rate Books

I use a tough 1–10 scale. Higher scores are hard to earn. For each genre, I review a consistent set of questions across five pillars (like Originality, Structure, and Writing Quality). These questions guide my judgment; For each question I assign an answer of either Yes, No, or Somewhat. Each of these answers has a weight assigned to it and then from there I calculate the final score between 1-10.

Occassionally I will adjust the score manually if the questions don't produce an answer that feels right. But this is actually very rare - I've found that this system produces a satisfactory result nearly every time.

Novels 5 Pillars

Originality & Impact
  • Did the story feel fresh or unique in its themes or execution?
  • Did the book subvert or reinvent common tropes in an effective way?
  • Did anything meaningfully challenge your expectations or worldview?
  • Did the author’s voice or approach stand out from others in the genre?
  • Will you likely remember this book years from now because of its impact?
Development & Depth
  • Were the main characters multi-dimensional and believable?
  • Did character motivations feel complex and well explored?
  • Were relationships dynamic and developed with nuance?
  • Did the world feel immersive, with rich cultural or emotional layers?
  • Were deeper themes meaningfully integrated into the narrative?
Engagement & Enjoyment
  • Did the book hook you early in the story?
  • Were you eager to return to the book when away from it?
  • Did the story hold your interest throughout with minimal slow spots?
  • Did the emotional stakes feel real and compelling?
  • Did the book deliver moments of genuine joy, tension, or emotion?
Structure & Flow
  • Did the plot develop in a way that felt natural and earned?
  • Was there a strong sense of progression or rising tension?
  • Did the pacing feel well-balanced from beginning to end?
  • Were chapter transitions or shifts in time/place handled smoothly?
  • Did the story build to a satisfying or powerful conclusion?
Writing Quality
  • Was the prose vivid, evocative, or emotionally immersive?
  • Did the dialogue feel authentic and reveal character or theme?
  • Were metaphors, symbolism, or imagery used meaningfully?
  • Did the tone and style fit the genre and emotional stakes?
  • Did the writing elevate the story beyond just solid storytelling?

Non‑Fiction Books 5 Pillars

Insight & Perspective
  • Did the book present ideas or angles you hadn’t seen before?
  • Did it challenge or expand how you think about the world?
  • Did it make you curious to learn more about the topic afterward?
  • Did it leave you reflecting on its ideas after you put it down?
  • Did the book help you see familiar issues or topics in a new light?
Clarity & Understanding
  • Did the book explain its topic in a clear and thorough way?
  • Were key terms, events, or people introduced with enough background to follow?
  • Did the book help you understand something that once felt confusing or intimidating?
  • Were complex ideas broken down into manageable, understandable parts?
  • Did it explore different sides of a topic without feeling biased or shallow?
Engagement & Enjoyment
  • Did the book grab your attention early on?
  • Did it stay interesting throughout without dragging?
  • Did it use real stories, people, or events to keep things engaging?
  • Were there moments that surprised, moved, or intrigued you?
  • Did the book make you want to talk about it with someone?
Structure & Flow
  • Was the organization easy to follow from chapter to chapter?
  • Did each section build on the one before it?
  • Were ideas introduced at the right time, not all at once or out of order?
  • Did transitions between topics or chapters feel smooth?
  • Did the book feel cohesive and purposeful from beginning to end?
Writing Quality
  • Was the writing clear and free of confusing jargon?
  • Did the tone feel approachable and not overly dry or academic?
  • Were anecdotes, analogies, or metaphors used effectively to explain ideas?
  • Was the style consistent and easy to read throughout the book?
  • Did the writing help make the subject feel vivid, human, or emotionally resonant?

Children’s Books 5 Pillars

Message & Meaning
  • Did the book offer a meaningful message or insight relevant to middle-grade readers?
  • Did it explore themes like identity, friendship, fairness, courage, or belonging in a thoughtful way?
  • Did it encourage emotional growth, curiosity, or deeper thinking?
  • Was the moral or takeaway woven into the story without feeling forced or preachy?
  • Did the book leave you thinking or spark conversation after it ended?
Engagement & Appeal
  • Did the story capture your interest quickly and hold it throughout?
  • Were there moments of excitement, tension, humor, or surprise?
  • Did the story feel imaginative, adventurous, or emotionally rich?
  • Was the pacing appropriate for independent readers (not too slow or too intense)?
  • Would the intended age group likely recommend it to friends?
Characters & Relatability
  • Were the main characters believable, interesting, and age-appropriate?
  • Did the characters face challenges that felt real and meaningful to their stage of life?
  • Were the emotional arcs — such as fear, bravery, or self-discovery — well developed?
  • Did the characters grow, learn, or change in ways readers could connect with?
  • Were different backgrounds, family situations, or cultures presented with empathy and care?
Plot & Structure
  • Did the story follow a clear and satisfying arc (beginning, middle, climax, resolution)?
  • Did each chapter or section contribute meaningfully to the whole?
  • Were there logical transitions and consistent progression without confusion?
  • Was the conflict or mystery engaging enough to drive the story forward?
  • Did the ending feel earned, satisfying, or emotionally resonant?
Writing Quality
  • Was the vocabulary appropriate but still challenging and interesting for this age group?
  • Did the prose flow smoothly and feel well-crafted without being overly complex?
  • Was the tone respectful of the reader’s intelligence and emotional range?
  • Did the writing evoke vivid imagery, strong emotions, or immersive moments?
  • Did the author show creativity or voice that stood out in the genre?

Short Stories 5 Pillars

Concept & Impact
  • Did the story or collection present a striking or memorable concept?
  • Did it explore an idea or emotional truth in a fresh or compelling way?
  • Did it leave a strong impression on you after finishing it?
  • Did it make you pause, reflect, or reread sections due to depth or intrigue?
  • Did it say something meaningful or provocative in a short space?
Character & Emotion
  • Did the characters feel emotionally real despite the short length?
  • Were you able to care about or empathize with the characters quickly?
  • Did the relationships or inner conflicts feel believable or moving?
  • Were motivations and choices conveyed clearly, without heavy exposition?
  • Did the character(s) change, reveal something deep, or stay with you?
Engagement & Atmosphere
  • Did the story grab your attention from the first paragraph?
  • Did the tone, pacing, or stakes feel intense or absorbing?
  • Was the atmosphere or mood vividly conveyed?
  • Did you feel emotionally or mentally “immersed” while reading?
  • Were there moments of surprise, beauty, tension, or resonance?
Structure & Execution
  • Was the structure (linear, fragmented, framed, etc.) effective for the story?
  • Did the ending feel earned — whether conclusive, open-ended, or ambiguous?
  • Was the story or collection cohesive and thoughtfully arranged?
  • Did each story in a collection feel distinctive yet thematically aligned?
  • Did the author make strong use of the short form (e.g., compression, implication, restraint)?
Writing Quality
  • Was the prose tight, purposeful, and artfully crafted?
  • Did the language evoke emotion, imagery, or insight without excess?
  • Were literary devices (symbolism, metaphor, irony) used effectively?
  • Did the style fit the emotional weight or themes of the piece?
  • Did the voice feel confident, distinct, or original?

Plays & Legends/Lore 5 Pillars

Concept & Impact
  • Did the work present a striking or memorable concept, theme, or mythic idea?
  • Did it explore universal truths, moral lessons, or cultural values in a fresh or compelling way?
  • Did it leave a strong impression on you after finishing/experiencing it?
  • Did it provoke reflection, deeper questioning, or re-reading/re-watching to grasp its layers?
  • Did it communicate something meaningful, provocative, or timeless within its form?
Character, Voice & Emotion
  • Did the characters (or archetypes) feel emotionally real, symbolic, or resonant?
  • Were you able to care about, empathize with, or be intrigued by the characters quickly?
  • Did the relationships, conflicts, or symbolic roles feel believable, moving, or thematically rich?
  • Were motivations, choices, and consequences conveyed clearly without excessive exposition?
  • Did the characters (or symbolic figures) change, reveal something deep, or stay with you after?
Engagement, Atmosphere & Performance
  • Did the opening (or prologue/oral hook) immediately capture your attention?
  • Did the pacing, rhythm, or stakes feel engaging—whether read, performed, or told aloud?
  • Was the atmosphere, setting, or world vividly conveyed?
  • Did you feel immersed—emotionally, imaginatively, or communally—in the story?
  • Were there moments of surprise, beauty, tension, or resonance that stood out?
Structure, Tradition & Execution
  • Was the chosen structure (linear, episodic, cyclical, framed, oral formulaic) effective for the story?
  • Did the ending feel earned—whether conclusive, ambiguous, or open to interpretation?
  • Was the work cohesive and thoughtfully arranged, even if part of a larger tradition or cycle?
  • Did individual scenes, episodes, or tales feel distinctive yet aligned with the whole?
  • Did the work make strong use of its medium (brevity in short stories, dialogue/action in plays, oral compression/repetition in legends/lore)?
Language, Style & Cultural Resonance
  • Was the language tight, purposeful, or artfully crafted (whether prose, verse, or oral cadence)?
  • Did the style evoke emotion, imagery, or insight without excess?
  • Were literary or performative devices (metaphor, irony, chorus, repetition, symbolism) used effectively?
  • Did the style and voice fit the emotional weight, cultural context, or themes of the piece?
  • Did the voice feel confident, distinct, or enduring across time and retelling?
