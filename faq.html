Frequently Asked Questions

When I first started rating books, it was all gut feeling—just a number that felt right. But over time, I wanted to be more fair and consistent. Now I use a system with very detailed questions that I answer after every book. It helps me break things down and really think about what worked and what didn’t. I got inspired by Dave Portnoy’s pizza reviews. I appreciate that his scale is tough, and a high score actually means something. I wanted the same for books. That’s why you won’t see many perfect scores from me. I’ve never given a 10. I’m still waiting to read one.

People often ask how I read so many books. Part of it is that I’ve always been a fast reader—it just comes naturally. But the real secret is something called immersive reading. I listen to the audiobook while reading the physical (or Kindle) version at the same time. Over time, I’ve trained myself to handle faster playback speeds without losing much comprehension. It’s not perfect—I still get distracted sometimes and have to backtrack—but it works. I also bring audiobooks with me everywhere. In the car, doing dishes, folding laundry... all those little moments really add up to more reading time.
